Matt Meselson at an Ultra-Centrifuge, c. mid 1950s

Photograph courtesy of the Archives, California Institute of Technology


"There remained a single missing piece in the double helical jigsaw puzzle: our unzipping idea for DNA replication had yet to be experimentally verified...Matt Meselson and Frank Stahl put to rest such fears when they published the results of a single elegant experiment. They had met in the summer of 1954 at the MBL and agreed - over a good many gin martinis - that they should get together to do some science. The result of their collaboration has been described as "one of the most beautiful experiments in biology."

James Watson
